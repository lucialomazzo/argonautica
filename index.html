Dissertation Project

Argonautica

A multicultural study of religion, ritual, and identity in the voyage of Jason and the Argonauts.

Explore the Study

About the Dissertation

Argonautica examines how ancient communities used myth to think about sacred power, social belonging, and intercultural contact. Rather than reading the poem as a single, isolated tradition, this project places Greek, Near Eastern, Egyptian, and Mediterranean religious ideas in conversation.

Core Research Question

How does the Argonautica represent religious practice when heroes cross cultural borders, and what do these representations reveal about ancient pluralism?

Method

  • Close reading of key episodes in the poem.
  • Comparative analysis with ritual texts, hymns, and cult evidence.
  • Attention to multilingual and multicultural encounters across the voyage.

Religious Themes

  • Divine mediation and the role of prophecy.
  • Sacrifice, purification, and sacred law.
  • Gender, charisma, and priestly authority (especially Medea).
  • Foreign sanctuaries and ritual exchange.

Why It Matters

This dissertation argues that the Argonautica is not only an adventure narrative but also a map of ancient religious imagination. The voyage reveals how myth can preserve differences while also creating shared symbolic worlds between cultures.
